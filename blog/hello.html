I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU) within the Energy Markets and Analytics section, supervised by Prof. Jalal Kazempour (DTU) and Prof. Pierre Pinson (Imperial College London).

My research interests include mechanism design, forecasting and the economics of data. This generally inolves problems at the intersection of machine learning, optimization and game theory.

I hold an M.Sc. in Data Science and Energy Systems from University College London (Energy and Artifical Intelligence Lab), where I wrote my thesis in collaboration with Invenia Labs, supervised by Dr. Letif Mones.

Before starting my Ph.D., I worked within the Data Science team at Arenko Group developing probabilistic models to forecast wholesale electricity prices and optimization algorthims to trade flexible assets (e.g., batteries).
